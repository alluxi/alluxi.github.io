Rule of law and equal rights

Written on Jun 27, 2015

The rule of law is paramount in a democracy. In European thinking there is a similar term called rechtsstaat. When the government adhere to these principles, it is not only about having its laws current, but also about having just laws, which are morally right.

This bring me to the topic of this post – equal rights. This week the United Sates’ Supreme Court ruled that same-sex marriage is a right every American have, regardless of their state and its legislation concerning the matter.

The Supreme Court could rule on this because of the 14th amendment of the US Constitution. It stipulates that everyone have “the equal protection of the law”. Equal rights are one of the cornerstones of a rechtsstaat.

It is sad that battles like this are up to nine judges and not the representatives that are elected by the people for this very job. I have already pointed out some of the flaws the American electoral system faces, and this just emphasises it.

At least this is a step in the right direction.

Governments for citizens

Written on Jun 19, 2015

The US presidential election campaign has begun, as you already know. One of the most irritating aspects of the entire campaign process are financial muscles. It appears to the public that candidates are open for purchase. This is not favourable, nor is it fitting.

The gap between the politicians and the people they are sat to govern must not great. The result is contempt among the common person against the rule. This alone is an argument for restricting this kind of practice in the campaign process.

Donald Trump said in his speech that he was too rich for purchase. That is solving the problem by the wrong means. It is simply sending the wrong signals. Politics ought to be open for everyone to enter.

Maybe are the entire US political system up for debate. This kind of problems are non-existent in the European political landscape, and as an added bonus: more than two parties.

The system is flawed – reforms are needed. In the end, the government is for its citizens, not the other way around.

A ground-breaking candidacy

Written on Jun 07, 2015

Bernie Sanders is a self-declared democratic socialist, not too much of a difference from social liberalism, and he is running for president in the US. That is just astonishing.

One of the main issues he is advocating are income and wealth inequalities. Some would go as far as to say this is his main objective.

On his campaign website, he uses the phrase “economic and political oligarchy”. A phrase we often hear in regards to Russia and their political system and distribution of wealth. He is really onto something here. The more I think about the statement, I come to realise it is very much applicable to the American society.

The middle class is going extinct, as he quite rightly so remarks. The ridiculously wealthy always replies with “someone needs to create jobs”. That is correct, even the former Norwegian Secretary of International Development, Erik Solheim, has admitted, “Someone needs to get rich before we can lift the masses”. Nota bene; Erik Solheim is a party member of the Norwegian Socialist Left Party.

Nevertheless, this is not an excuse to get filthy rich and go fighting against general prosperity. This is a key fact Bernie Sanders has noticed. After the wealthy have created the jobs, the wealth redistribution must happen.

Sanders is also talking about universal health care. I cannot agree more. The government’s objective and goal is to make its citizens prosper. This includes having access to medical services – free of charge or at least at a rate everyone can afford.

Good luck, Bernie Sanders!

Taking care of the elderly

Written on May 02, 2015

An important element when discussing what the society can do for the elderly is public pension schemes. When every worker pays a fixed percentage of the salary every month to the government, they will benefit from a guaranteed pension later on.

Those who do not have a steady job will receive the minimum pension rate from the government. This ensures everyone a dignified life of retirement. The rate must from time to time be adjusted for inflation.

Nursing homes

Providing nursing homes for the elderly ought to be a public service. The elderly must be kept busy in order for them to be happy. This can be archived by making sure those of the elderly that want it, can establish an activity club.

To lift some of the financial burden off the state and at the same time give the elderly a more dignified life, new and existing technology must be taken in use to let the elderly live home as long as they want and are able to.
